**Practical Application:** Stick to your chosen timeframe and ignore external market movements outside your system.

- **Consistency Required:** Once chosen, stick to your rule consistently
- **Learning Phase:** When learning, don't worry about external factors

**Flexibility Options:**
- **3 Minutes:** Standard rule for most conditions

- **Annual Volume:** 3-4 trades daily Ã— 365 days = 1,095+ annual opportunities
- **No FOMO Needed:** Missing one day's trade is not critical

**Mindset:** Focus on consistent execution rather than catching every single opportunity.

#### 4. Recent Evidence
**Why This Rule:** Different timeframes have proven effective in recent market conditions.

**Evidence Examples:**
- **Last Week:** 3-minute rule worked successfully
- **This Thursday:** 10-minute rule worked for reversals
- **Variable Success:** Different timeframes work in different market conditions

**Adaptation:** Be prepared to adjust your timeframe based on current market behavior.

### Practical Guidelines

#### Decision Framework
1. **Choose Your Timeframe:** Select 3, 5, or 10 minutes based on your trading style
2. **Stick to the Rule:** Apply your chosen timeframe consistently
3. **Ignore External Noise:** Don't worry about opportunities outside your timeframe
4. **Focus on Execution:** Prioritize consistent rule application over catching everything

#### Market Context Considerations
- **Down Markets:** Consider extending to 5-10 minutes for reversal confirmation

- **Volatile Markets:** May benefit from extended timeframes

#### Risk Management
- **Consistency Over Perfection:** Better to be consistently right than occasionally perfect
- **Opportunity Cost:** Time spent worrying about missed trades reduces focus on current opportunities
- **System Integrity:** Maintaining rule consistency preserves system effectiveness

### Common Mistakes to Avoid

1. **Timeframe Hopping:** Switching between different timeframes based on recent results
2. **External Distraction:** Worrying about opportunities in other markets or timeframes
3. **FOMO Trading:** Chasing trades outside your established rules

### Conclusion

The choice of timeframe (3, 5, or 10 minutes) is less important than consistent application of your chosen rule. In down markets where strong starts may not sustain, consider using a longer timeframe (5-10 minutes) for better confirmation, but maintain consistency once your choice is made. Remember that trading opportunities are abundant, so missing one trade is not critical to long-term success.

## Q2: How/when to enter if there is no strong start?

### Answer 
Strong start is the number one ingredient to look for in a trade. If there's no strong start, it's essentially a "week stock" and should be avoided. Trading time constraints determine what opportunities you can realistically pursue, so focus only on what fits within your system and timeframe. Don't worry about opportunities outside your defined parameters, just as you wouldn't worry about crypto, gold, or Forex markets if they don't fit your trading approach.

#### Key Principles
- **Core principle:** Strong start requirement for trade selection
- **Practical application:** When no strong start is present, avoid the trade
- **Timeframe considerations:** 80-20 rule and trading time limitations
- **System focus:** Stay within your defined trading parameters
- **Conclusion:** Work within your constraints and ignore external distractions

#### 1. Core Principle: Strong Start is Essential
**Why This Rule:** Strong start is the number one ingredient to look for in a trade.

- Why is strong start essential for trade selection? Strong start indicates institutional interest and momentum needed for profitable trades
- If there's no strong start, it's essentially a "week stock" that lacks the strength needed for profitable trades, so it's better to avoid such setups entirely
- As trading weak stocks goes against the fundamental principle of capturing strong moves, making such trades invalid, nobody wants to trade weak stocks right?

**Practical Application:** Avoid entering trades without strong starts as they represent weak stock behavior.

#### 2. Practical Application: When No Strong Start
**Why This Rule:** If there's no strong start in your timeframe, don't trade.

- If there's no strong start, avoid the trade entirely since weak stocks don't provide the momentum needed for profitable setups
- Trading without strong start means trading weak stocks, which is invalid because the goal is to capture strong moves, not weak ones
- Focus on quality setups that meet your strong start criteria rather than forcing trades in weak conditions

**Practical Application:** Wait for proper strong start setups within your trading window.

#### 3. Timeframe Considerations: 80-20 Rule
**Why This Rule:** Trading time constraints determine what opportunities you can realistically pursue.
- Since 80% of money comes from the first hour, it makes sense to focus your trading efforts within that critical timeframe
- If you have limited trading time due to a morning job, concentrate only on opportunities within the first hour when the market is most active
- When you can only trade the first hour, it's logical to focus only on opportunities within that timeframe rather than worrying about later moves

**Practical Application:** Concentrate your trading efforts within your available timeframe.

#### 4. System Focus: Stay Within Your Parameters
**Why This Rule:** Don't worry about opportunities outside your trading system or timeframe.

- Don't worry about what happens at 11, 12, 1, 3 if it's outside your trading window since you can't act on those opportunities anyway
- If your job doesn't allow trading after the first hour, ignore later opportunities because they're outside your system's scope
- Just like you wouldn't worry about crypto, gold, or Forex markets if they don't fit your trading approach, ignore opportunities outside your defined parameters

**Practical Application:** Stay focused on your defined trading window and ignore external distractions.

#### 5. Conclusion: Work Within Your Constraints
**Why This Rule:** Apply consistent rules based on your trading limitations.

- Find trades in the first hour if that's your limitation since that's when the best opportunities typically occur
- If you can't trade after the first hour, don't concern yourself with later developments because they're outside your actionable timeframe
- Trade only within your defined parameters and timeframe to maintain system integrity and avoid overextending yourself

**Practical Application:** Focus on finding strong starts within your available trading timeframe and avoid opportunities outside your system or time constraints.

## Q3: Could you please explain 5% within 20 days high scanner?

### Answer Summary

The 5% within 20 days high scanner identifies stocks that are approaching their recent highs, indicating potential uptrends. When a stock that previously reached 100 drops to 80, then climbs back to 95, it's within 5% of its 20-day high, suggesting the stock is regaining momentum and potentially entering an uptrend. This forms a "cup" pattern that signals a good entry opportunity near recent resistance levels.

#### Key Principles
- **Uptrend Identification:** Stocks within 5% of 20-day highs show momentum recovery
- **Magic Number Flexibility:** 5% and 20 days are not fixed - can be adjusted based on preference
- **System Consistency:** Stick to your chosen parameters for consistent results
- **Probability-Based:** Scanner increases chances of finding profitable setups, not guarantees
- **Combination Approach:** Works best as part of a broader trading system
